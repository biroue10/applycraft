This page explains the cookies and browser storage ApplyCraft relies on. The core builder is designed to avoid tracking cookies — but it is not accurate to say "no cookies at all," because our security provider and optional features use a small number of necessary technologies.

1. Browser storage for preferences, not document autosave

ApplyCraft does not automatically store your résumé or cover-letter content in localStorage. Form data is cleared when you reopen the builder, unless you explicitly use a feature that stores or shares the document. Non-sensitive preferences and optional account state may still be kept in browser storage. Keys we use include:

  • ac_site_language, ac_interface_language, ac_document_language — language preferences
  • ac_subscription, ac_session, ac_account, ac_consent_sync — optional account / pass state

2. Analytics cookies

We use Google Analytics 4 to understand aggregated product usage (for example, "a resume was exported"). It sets first-party cookies — _ga and _ga_<id> — which persist for up to two years and are used to recognise returning visits. We do not send resume content, names, or email addresses to Google Analytics. See the Privacy Policy and Google's privacy policy.

Google Analytics only runs if you accept it in the cookie banner shown on your first visit. Rejecting takes one click and keeps analytics fully off. You can change your choice at any time using Cookie preferences in the footer.

These are security cookies set by our hosting/CDN provider. They are not used for advertising or cross-site profiling. The payment provider (Lemon Squeezy) may set its own cookies on its checkout pages, governed by its policy.

4. Managing cookies and storage

You can block or delete cookies in your browser settings and clear ApplyCraft's local storage with the in-app "Delete local data" control. Blocking strictly necessary security cookies may affect the site's ability to protect against abuse.
