How to write a registered nurse resume

Nursing resumes are screened for licences, certifications, and clinical specialties first — make them impossible to miss, then back them with measurable patient-care outcomes.

🪪

Licences & Credentials First

Place your RN licence, state, and certifications (BLS, ACLS, PALS) in the header so they're seen immediately by recruiters and ATS.

🏥

Specify Your Specialty & Setting

Med/Surg, ICU, ER, telemetry, oncology — and the unit size, patient ratio, and acuity you handled.

📊

Quantify Patient Care

Patient load per shift, medication accuracy, satisfaction (HCAHPS), and improvement initiatives turn duties into achievements.

💻

List EHR Systems

Epic, Cerner, Meditech — electronic health record proficiency is a common explicit requirement.

🎓

Education & Continuing Ed

Your BSN/ADN, plus any continuing education or specialty courses, signal commitment to clinical excellence.

✅

Keep Formatting ATS-Safe

Hospitals rely heavily on ATS. Use clear headings and avoid tables or graphics that can scramble your credentials.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should a registered nurse resume include?

Your RN licence and state, certifications (BLS, ACLS, PALS), a clinical summary, specialties and care settings, quantified patient-care achievements, EHR systems, and your nursing degree.

How do I write a new-grad nursing resume with little experience?

Lead with your BSN/ADN, licence, and certifications, then highlight clinical rotations, preceptorships, capstone projects, and any patient-facing or healthcare support roles. Include relevant skills and EHR exposure.

Should I list my nursing licence number on my resume?

List the licence type, state, and that it is active — but you generally do not need to publish the full licence number on a resume. Provide it through the employer's secure verification process if requested.

How long should a registered nurse resume be?

One page for new grads and early-career nurses; two pages is acceptable for experienced RNs with multiple specialties, certifications, and significant clinical history.
